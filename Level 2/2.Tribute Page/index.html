Biography

Cristiano Ronaldo, born February 5, 1985, in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal, is a legendary footballer known for his exceptional skills, speed, and goal-scoring prowess. He began his career at Sporting CP before moving to Manchester United in 2003, where he became a global star under Sir Alex Ferguson. In 2009, he joined Real Madrid, winning numerous trophies including four Champions League titles. In 2018, Ronaldo transferred to Juventus, where he continued to excel in Serie A. Internationally, he has led Portugal to victory in the 2016 European Championship and the 2019 Nations League. Off the field, Ronaldo is a philanthropist and a global icon known for his business ventures and endorsements. His legacy as one of the greatest footballers of all time is secured by his numerous individual awards and records.
